Nahar International School

NIS is a school in Powai, Mumbai. It is English medium and has various facilities. I am very happy to be a part of it as it has wonderful teachers and teaching methods which benefit students very much. Sports, academics, extra curriculars, it has everything. We get maximum 9 classes a day with 2 breaks but of course COVID school timings are different. The campus is considerably large and spacious.

campus

The Cafeteria

2nd Floor

Our cafeteria is very large and each class has its own table to sit at. There is a counter where we can go get food or we can bring food from home as well. There are facts about veggies and fruits written on the walls for information and it is very colourful and bright.

The Library

3rd Floor

Our library is also very large. It has sections of shelves ordered grade wise so that we all read age-appropriate books. Our librarians help us choose our books, borrow and return them. There are tables and chairs to sit and read and we even do group projects while sitting in the library.

The Turf

Ground Floor

The turf is an oval shaped playing ground that we use to play many sports and activities on. It has goal posts for handball and football, basketball nets for basketball and tracks for running.The school lends the turf to other companies after school hours for sports purposes as well.

What's New?

  • the "After School Help" system

    The "After School Help" program was made to help students who are not able to cope or understand the work being taught in class. As times per class are limited, some students cannot voice their queries or comprehend the task at hand. So, our teachers are taking 45 minutes to 1 hour of coaching our students in various subjects. The teachers choose which subjects a particular student needs help in and coach them daily, improving their learning experience!

  • The Child Connect Meetings

    In the primary section of the school, the Child Connect program has been introduced. Here, our teachers personally talk to the students about how they are doing and what they need help with. It is unlike the After School Help system as this is rather like a free and easy talk or "connect" with the teacher while the ASH was a coaching session. As the students in primary are younger, they may require more help and guidance from their teachers.

  • The Online Student Council

    The Online Student Council is something that did have its counterpart in Non-Virtual school as well. In this case, the Online counterpart takes initiatives in a Virtual way by organizing competetions and activites and many more things that might improve online school life. Teachers of course do aid, but our students dominate this field! We have already had an Inter-House Online Walkathon and many more events are yet to come!
